# Contemporary deliverance in Christianity

This survey grew out of a desire to understand the common teachings of contemporary deliverance ministries, and the reasoning behind them.

It isn't about casting demons out of someone who isn't filled with the Holy Spirit — that's not in dispute. It's specifically about ministries that teach a Christian can be "demonized." As the [word study in Christians and Deliverance Ministry](christians-and-deliverance.md#the-key-term-demonized) covers in more detail, that's largely a choice of English gloss for the same underlying claim the term "demon possession" would otherwise carry, not a distinct, milder condition the Greek text actually supports.

What follows is a collection of quotes from specific contemporary deliverance ministries, gathered by search rather than exhaustive survey. The goal is direct quotation, not editorial assessment — the reader can weigh these for themselves. Two representative lines, without attribution to any one ministry below, give a sense of the register this teaching is often couched in:

> He may speak things that seem strange and unusual to you, but it is the Holy Spirit.
>
> You are receiving a fresher and newer anointing.

Overall, time is better spent reading God's Word directly. This survey is offered for anyone for whom one of these specific ministries is already an active influence.

## Rob Reimer

Reimer's "soul care" framework. Recurring language includes "more of God" and "it's all about life change."

### Seeking authority

Soul care's seventh principle is deliverance, framed around developing personal spiritual authority:

> Consider authority. Jesus cast out demons by authority. Jesus gave his disciples authority to drive out demons and heal the sick (Matthew 10:1). Authority is developmental. Are you developing authority? Is it demonstrated in your life? Are you utilizing the authority that you have developed to advance the Kingdom of God? How can you develop your authority further? (Note: Dr Rob Reimer's book *Spiritual Authority* looks at how to develop spiritual authority and can be a useful resource on this subject.) What practical action steps are you going to take to intentionally grow in spiritual authority? Read a book, like *Spiritual Authority*. Join a conference like the Deliverance Training Workshop. Join a ministry team in your local church. Dialog with the Holy Spirit to see if He invites you into anything specific.

## Conclusion

Across every ministry surveyed here, the same underlying pattern recurs: an appeal to "more" available from God than what is already fully accomplished in Christ, and a technical vocabulary ("demonized," "soul ties," "authority," "sin energy") that reframes ordinary Christian categories — sin, forgiveness, temptation — as things requiring a specialized session or practitioner to resolve. See [Christians and Deliverance Ministry](christians-and-deliverance.md) for the biblical case against a believer being "demonized" in the first place, word study included.
