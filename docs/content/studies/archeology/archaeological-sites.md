# Archaeological Sites Validating Scripture

This study examines significant archaeological sites that corroborate biblical accounts. Each entry includes relevant Scripture references, historical dating, and findings from excavations.

## Old Testament Sites

### Conquest and Settlement Period Sites

#### 1. Jericho (Tell es-Sultan)
**Location:** West Bank
**Excavation:** Various expeditions 1907-present
**Biblical Period:** c. 1400 BC (conquest)

**Findings:**
- Ancient walls dating to various periods
- Evidence of destruction layers
- Debate over dating (Bronze Age vs. Iron Age)
- One of oldest continuously inhabited cities
- Archaeological complexity due to multiple occupation layers

**Biblical Account:**
- Joshua 6:1-27 - Walls fell after 7 days of marching
- Rahab's house spared
- City devoted to destruction and cursed

**Scripture References:**
- Joshua 6:20 - "When the trumpets sounded, the army shouted, and at the sound of the trumpet, when the men gave a loud shout, the wall collapsed"
- Joshua 6:24 - "Then they burned the whole city and everything in it"
- 1 Kings 16:34 - Curse on rebuilder fulfilled

**Time Period:** Late Bronze Age conquest (c. 1400 BC per early exodus dating)

---

#### 2. Hazor
**Location:** Northern Israel
**Excavation:** 1950s-present (Yigael Yadin, Amnon Ben-Tor)
**Biblical Period:** Various (conquest through monarchy)

**Findings:**
- Massive Canaanite city (largest in Canaan, 200 acres)
- Destruction layer from c. 1230 BC (or c. 1400 BC depending on chronology)
- Israelite settlement above Canaanite layer
- Solomon's six-chambered gate (identical to Megiddo and Gezer)
- Water system similar to Megiddo
- Canaanite palace and temples

**Biblical Account:**
- Joshua 11:10-13 - Joshua burned Hazor
- Judges 4:2 - Jabin king of Hazor oppressed Israel
- 1 Kings 9:15 - Solomon fortified Hazor

**Scripture References:**
- Joshua 11:10-11 - "Joshua turned back and captured Hazor and put its king to the sword. (Hazor had been the head of all these kingdoms.) Everyone in it they put to the sword...and they burned Hazor itself"
- 1 Kings 9:15 - "King Solomon conscripted laborers to build the LORD's temple...and the wall of Jerusalem, and Hazor, Megiddo and Gezer"

**Archaeological Note:** The identical gate structures at Hazor, Megiddo, and Gezer validate 1 Kings 9:15's claim that Solomon fortified all three cities.

**Time Period:** Late Bronze Age through Iron Age (c. 1400-586 BC)

---

#### 3. Megiddo (Armageddon)
**Location:** Northern Israel (Jezreel Valley)
**Excavation:** 1903-present
**Biblical Period:** Multiple periods (Bronze Age through biblical times)

**Findings:**
- 26 layers of civilization
- Solomon's six-chambered gate (10th century BC)
- Administrative buildings and stables/storehouses
- Massive water system (shaft and tunnel reaching spring)
- Strategic military location controlling Via Maris trade route
- Canaanite temples and fortifications

**Biblical Account:**
- Joshua 12:21 - King of Megiddo conquered by Joshua
- Judges 5:19 - Battle site in Deborah's time
- 1 Kings 9:15 - Fortified by Solomon
- 2 Kings 23:29-30 - Josiah killed at Megiddo
- Revelation 16:16 - Final battle at Armageddon

**Scripture References:**
- Judges 5:19 - "Kings came, they fought...at Taanach by the waters of Megiddo"
- 1 Kings 9:15 - Solomon's building projects
- 2 Kings 23:29 - "Pharaoh Necho king of Egypt went up to the Euphrates River to help the king of Assyria. King Josiah marched out to meet him in battle, but Necho faced him and killed him at Megiddo"
- Revelation 16:16 - "Then they gathered the kings together to the place that in Hebrew is called Armageddon" (Har Megiddo = Mountain of Megiddo)

**Time Period:** Bronze Age through Biblical periods and prophetic future

### United and Divided Kingdom Sites

#### 4. The City of David (Jerusalem)
**Location:** Jerusalem (original Jebusite city south of Temple Mount)
**Excavation:** Ongoing (19th century-present)
**Biblical Period:** c. 1000 BC - present

**Findings:**
- Large stone structure (possibly David's palace or fortress)
- Stepped stone structure (10th century BC) - massive support system
- Bullae (seal impressions) with biblical names
- Warren's Shaft water system (pre-Israelite)
- Gihon Spring fortifications
- Sections of ancient walls and towers
- Evidence of Babylonian destruction (586 BC)

**Biblical Account:**
- 2 Samuel 5:6-10 - David conquers Jerusalem from Jebusites
- Made it capital of united Israel
- David's palace built here
- Site eventually expanded to include Temple Mount

**Scripture References:**
- 2 Samuel 5:7 - "David captured the fortress of Zion—which is the City of David"
- 2 Samuel 5:9 - "David then took up residence in the fortress and called it the City of David. He built up the area around it, from the terraces inward"
- 2 Samuel 5:11 - "Now Hiram king of Tyre sent envoys to David, along with cedar logs and carpenters and stonemasons, and they built a palace for David"
- 1 Kings 2:10 - "David rested with his ancestors and was buried in the City of David"

**Time Period:** United Monarchy period (c. 1000 BC onward)

#### 5. Gezer
**Location:** Central Israel
**Excavation:** 1902-present
**Biblical Period:** Bronze Age through biblical times

**Findings:**
- Six-chambered gate identical to Hazor and Megiddo (Solomon's)
- Canaanite high place with standing stones
- Evidence of Egyptian control (Amarna period)
- Palace and fortifications
- Gezer Calendar (10th century BC agricultural calendar in Hebrew)

**Biblical Account:**
- Joshua 10:33 - King of Gezer defeated
- Joshua 16:10 - Canaanites not driven out
- 1 Kings 9:15-17 - Solomon fortified Gezer (received from Pharaoh as dowry)

**Scripture References:**
- Joshua 10:33 - "Meanwhile, Horam king of Gezer had come up to help Lachish, but Joshua defeated him and his army"
- 1 Kings 9:16 - "Pharaoh king of Egypt had attacked and captured Gezer. He had set it on fire. He killed its Canaanite inhabitants and then gave it as a wedding gift to his daughter, Solomon's wife"
- 1 Kings 9:15 - Listed with Hazor and Megiddo as Solomon's building projects

**Time Period:** Bronze Age through Iron Age

### Jerusalem Water Systems

#### 6. Hezekiah's Tunnel and the Pool of Siloam
**Location:** Jerusalem (City of David)
**Discovery Date:** 1838 (modern rediscovery); Pool rediscovered 2004
**Biblical Period:** 8th century BC construction; 1st century AD use

**Findings:**

**Hezekiah's Tunnel:**
- 1,750-foot tunnel cut through solid rock
- Connected Gihon Spring to Pool of Siloam
- Siloam Inscription describes construction (two teams meeting)
- Engineering marvel of ancient world
- Built to secure water during Assyrian siege

**Pool of Siloam:**
- Large ritual bath (mikveh) from Second Temple period
- Stone steps leading into pool
- Fed by Hezekiah's Tunnel
- Used for ceremonial washing by pilgrims

**Biblical Account:**
- 2 Kings 20:20 - Hezekiah's water project
- 2 Chronicles 32:2-4, 30 - Built during Assyrian threat
- John 9:1-7 - Jesus heals blind man at Pool of Siloam

**Scripture References:**
- 2 Kings 20:20 - "As for the other events of Hezekiah's reign, all his achievements and how he made the pool and the tunnel by which he brought water into the city, are they not written in the book of the annals of the kings of Judah?"
- 2 Chronicles 32:30 - "It was Hezekiah who blocked the upper outlet of the Gihon spring and channeled the water down to the west side of the City of David"
- John 9:7 - "'Go,' he told him, 'wash in the Pool of Siloam' (this word means 'Sent'). So the man went and washed, and came home seeing"
- Isaiah 8:6 - "Because this people has rejected the gently flowing waters of Shiloah"

**Time Period:** First Temple construction (c. 701 BC) / Second Temple use (1st century AD)

---

#### 7. Pool of Bethesda
**Location:** Jerusalem, near Sheep Gate (northeast of Temple Mount)
**Discovery:** Excavated 19th-20th centuries
**Biblical Period:** Second Temple period (Jesus' ministry)

**Findings:**
- Large double pool complex (53m x 96m total)
- Five porticoes/colonnades (exactly as John's Gospel describes)
- Used for ritual bathing (mikveh)
- Possibly associated with healing (Asclepius cult or folk belief)
- Frescoes and graffiti from Byzantine period showing Christian veneration
- Remains of Byzantine and Crusader churches built over site

**Biblical Account:**
- John 5:1-9 - Jesus heals paralytic at Pool of Bethesda
- Man had been invalid for 38 years
- Jesus asks if he wants to be healed

**Scripture Reference:**
- John 5:2-4 - "Now there is in Jerusalem near the Sheep Gate a pool, which in Aramaic is called Bethesda and which is surrounded by five covered colonnades. Here a great number of disabled people used to lie—the blind, the lame, the paralyzed"

**Archaeological Note:** Critics once claimed this pool didn't exist. Discovery validated precise details in John's Gospel, including the unusual feature of five porticoes.

**Time Period:** Second Temple period, in use during Jesus' ministry (1st century AD)

### Destruction and Exile Sites

#### 8. Lachish
**Location:** Shephelah region, southwest of Jerusalem
**Excavation:** 1930s-present
**Biblical Period:** Multiple periods (conquest through exile)

**Findings:**
- Massive fortified city (second only to Jerusalem in Judah)
- Evidence of Assyrian siege and conquest (701 BC) - arrowheads, siege ramp
- Evidence of Babylonian destruction (586 BC)
- Lachish Letters (ostraca) from final days before Babylonian conquest
- Gate complex and palace
- Cult center with altar

**Biblical Account:**
- Joshua 10:31-32 - Conquered by Joshua
- 2 Kings 18:13-14 - Besieged by Sennacherib (701 BC)
- 2 Kings 25:1-2 - Fell to Babylonians
- Jeremiah 34:7 - One of last cities standing

**Scripture References:**
- Joshua 10:32 - "The LORD gave Lachish into Israel's hands, and Joshua took it on the second day"
- 2 Kings 18:13-14 - "In the fourteenth year of King Hezekiah's reign, Sennacherib king of Assyria attacked all the fortified cities of Judah and captured them"
- Jeremiah 34:7 - "while the army of the king of Babylon was fighting against Jerusalem and the other cities of Judah that were still holding out—Lachish and Azekah. These were the only fortified cities left in Judah"

**Archaeological Note:** Assyrian palace reliefs in Nineveh depict the siege of Lachish in detail, corroborating biblical and archaeological evidence.

**Time Period:** Late Bronze Age through Babylonian destruction (c. 1400-586 BC)

## New Testament Sites

### Sites from Jesus' Life and Ministry

#### 9. Nazareth Village and First-Century House
**Location:** Nazareth, Galilee
**Discovery Date:** 2009 (first-century house)
**Biblical Period:** First century AD (Jesus' childhood)

**Findings:**
- First-century house cut into limestone hillside
- Simple dwelling with courtyard
- Pottery, stone vessels, chalk artifacts
- Agricultural implements (wine press, olive press nearby)
- Proves Nazareth existed in Jesus' time (contra some skeptics)
- Evidence of small agricultural village

**Biblical Account:**
- Jesus grew up in Nazareth
- Called "Jesus of Nazareth"
- Rejected in His hometown synagogue

**Scripture References:**
- Luke 1:26-27 - "God sent the angel Gabriel to Nazareth, a town in Galilee, to a virgin pledged to be married to a man named Joseph"
- Matthew 2:23 - "and he went and lived in a town called Nazareth. So was fulfilled what was said through the prophets, that he would be called a Nazarene"
- Luke 4:16 - "He went to Nazareth, where he had been brought up, and on the Sabbath day he went into the synagogue, as was his custom"
- John 1:46 - "Nazareth! Can anything good come from there?"

**Time Period:** First century AD (Jesus' childhood, c. 4 BC - AD 26)

---

#### 10. Capernaum - The House of Peter
**Location:** Northern shore of Sea of Galilee
**Excavation:** 1968-present (Franciscan excavations)
**Biblical Period:** First century AD

**Findings:**
- First-century house identified as Peter's based on:
  - Early Christian graffiti mentioning "Peter," "Jesus," and Christian symbols
  - Transformation into house-church by mid-1st century
  - Later octagonal church built over it (5th century)
  - Fishing implements and simple pottery
  - Central location in ancient Capernaum
- Synagogue (4th century, built over 1st-century synagogue foundation)

**Biblical Account:**
- Matthew 8:14 - Jesus heals Peter's mother-in-law in his house
- Mark 2:1-12 - Paralytic lowered through roof (likely this house)
- Capernaum as Jesus' ministry headquarters ("his own city")

**Scripture References:**
- Matthew 8:14-15 - "When Jesus came into Peter's house, he saw Peter's mother-in-law lying in bed with a fever. He touched her hand and the fever left her"
- Mark 2:1 - "A few days later, when Jesus again entered Capernaum, the people heard that he had come home"
- Matthew 4:13 - "Leaving Nazareth, he went and lived in Capernaum, which was by the lake"
- Matthew 9:1 - "Jesus stepped into a boat, crossed over and came to his own town" (Capernaum)

**Time Period:** First century AD (ministry of Jesus, c. AD 27-30)

#### 11. Bethsaida (Et-Tell or El-Araj)
**Location:** Northern shore of Sea of Galilee
**Excavation:** Ongoing (two candidate sites debated)
**Biblical Period:** First century AD

**Findings:**
- First-century fishing village remains
- Remains of houses, fishing implements
- Later Roman city (Bethsaida Julias) built over it by Philip the Tetrarch
- Home of Peter, Andrew, and Philip

**Biblical Account:**
- Home of three apostles
- Site of multiple miracles
- Condemned by Jesus for unbelief

**Scripture References:**
- John 1:44 - "Philip, like Andrew and Peter, was from the town of Bethsaida"
- Mark 8:22-26 - Jesus heals blind man at Bethsaida
- Luke 9:10-17 - Feeding of 5,000 near Bethsaida
- Matthew 11:21 - "Woe to you, Bethsaida! For if the miracles that were performed in you had been performed in Tyre and Sidon, they would have repented long ago"

**Time Period:** First century AD

---

#### 12. Magdala (Migdal)
**Location:** Western shore of Sea of Galilee
**Discovery Date:** 2009 (synagogue discovered during hotel construction)
**Biblical Period:** First century AD

**Findings:**
- First-century synagogue (only synagogue from Jesus' time discovered in Galilee)
- "Magdala Stone" - carved stone with menorah and earliest representation of Second Temple
- Evidence of fishing industry and fish processing
- Ritual baths (mikvaot)
- Harbor installations
- Provides context for Jesus' Galilean ministry

**Biblical Account:**
- Home of Mary Magdalene
- Likely visited by Jesus during Galilean ministry

**Scripture References:**
- Luke 8:2 - "Mary (called Magdalene) from whom seven demons had come out"
- Matthew 27:56, 61 - Mary Magdalene at crucifixion and burial
- John 20:1-18 - Mary Magdalene first to see risen Jesus
- Mark 16:9 - "When Jesus rose early on the first day of the week, he appeared first to Mary Magdalene, out of whom he had driven seven demons"

**Archaeological Note:** The Magdala Stone is significant as the earliest carved representation of the Second Temple, created by someone who had actually seen it.

**Time Period:** First century AD

#### 13. Jacob's Well
**Location:** Nablus (ancient Shechem), West Bank
**Status:** Still exists and produces water today
**Biblical Period:** Patriarchal era / Jesus' ministry

**Findings:**
- Ancient well still producing water after nearly 4,000 years
- Over 100 feet deep
- Located at base of Mt. Gerizim
- Churches built over it (Byzantine, Crusader, modern Orthodox)
- At intersection of major north-south and east-west routes

**Biblical Account:**
- Dug by Jacob in patriarchal times
- Jesus meets Samaritan woman at the well
- Site of profound theological discourse

**Scripture References:**
- Genesis 33:18-19 - "After Jacob came from Paddan Aram, he arrived safely at the city of Shechem in Canaan and camped within sight of the city. For a hundred pieces of silver, he bought from the sons of Hamor, the father of Shechem, the plot of ground where he pitched his tent"
- Joshua 24:32 - "And Joseph's bones, which the Israelites had brought up from Egypt, were buried at Shechem in the tract of land that Jacob bought"
- John 4:5-6 - "So he came to a town in Samaria called Sychar, near the plot of ground Jacob had given to his son Joseph. Jacob's well was there, and Jesus, tired as he was from the journey, sat down by the well"
- John 4:7-26 - Extended dialogue about living water and worship

**Time Period:** Patriarchal age (c. 1800 BC) / Jesus' ministry (c. AD 27-30)

#### 14. The Galilee Boat ("Jesus Boat")
**Location:** Discovered in Sea of Galilee (during drought), now in museum
**Discovery Date:** 1986
**Biblical Period:** First century AD

**Findings:**
- 27-foot fishing boat from 1st century AD
- Carbon-dated to 100 BC - AD 70
- Same type Jesus and disciples would have used
- Preserved in mud for 2,000 years, carefully excavated
- Made of various wood types (repairs over lifetime)
- Could hold 13-15 people

**Biblical Context:**
- Illustrates Gospel accounts of Jesus in boat
- Shows fishing industry on Sea of Galilee
- Demonstrates biblical accuracy of boat descriptions
- Provides tangible connection to Gospel narratives

**Scripture References:**
- Mark 4:35-41 - Jesus calms storm: "That day when evening came, he said to his disciples, 'Let us go over to the other side.' Leaving the crowd behind, they took him along, just as he was, in the boat"
- Luke 5:1-11 - Miraculous catch of fish, calling disciples
- John 21:1-14 - Post-resurrection appearance with fishing
- Matthew 14:22-33 - Jesus walks on water, Peter steps out

**Time Period:** First century AD (contemporaneous with Jesus)

### Jerusalem - Crucifixion and Resurrection Sites

#### 15. The Temple Mount and Western Wall
**Location:** Jerusalem
**Status:** Continuously documented and visible
**Biblical Period:** Second Temple period (516 BC - AD 70)

**Findings:**
- Massive retaining wall built by Herod (begun 20 BC)
- Stones weighing up to 600 tons (largest hewn stones in ancient world)
- Robinson's Arch - remnant of monumental stairway to Temple Mount
- Southern Steps - pilgrims' entrance to Temple
- Mikvaot (ritual baths) at southern wall
- Warning inscription forbidding Gentiles from inner courts
- Shops and administrative buildings
- Evidence of Roman destruction (AD 70) - stones pushed down from walls

**Biblical Account:**
- Herod's Temple expansion
- Jesus taught here regularly
- Cleansed temple twice
- Predicted its destruction
- Early church worshiped here until AD 70

**Scripture References:**
- Mark 13:1-2 - "As Jesus was leaving the temple, one of his disciples said to him, 'Look, Teacher! What massive stones! What magnificent buildings!' 'Do you see all these great buildings?' replied Jesus. 'Not one stone here will be left on another; every one will be thrown down'"
- Matthew 24:1-2 - Parallel account of prophecy
- Luke 21:5-6 - "As for what you see here, the time will come when not one stone will be left on another; all will be thrown down"
- Luke 2:41-52 - Boy Jesus in the Temple
- John 2:13-22 - Jesus cleanses Temple (first time)
- Matthew 21:12-13 - Jesus cleanses Temple (second time)
- Acts 3:1-10 - Peter and John heal lame man at Beautiful Gate

**Fulfillment:** Destroyed August AD 70 by Romans under Titus, exactly as Jesus prophesied. Josephus records soldiers literally prying apart stones to get gold that melted in the fire.

**Time Period:** Second Temple period (516 BC - AD 70)

#### 16. Golgotha and Tomb Sites
**Location:** Jerusalem
**Identification:** Two traditional sites debated
**Biblical Period:** AD 30-33 (crucifixion and resurrection)

**Two Main Candidates:**

**Church of the Holy Sepulchre:**
- Archaeological evidence of 1st-century quarry (place of execution)
- Rock-cut tombs from Jesus' period discovered beneath church
- Venerated as crucifixion and burial site since at least 4th century
- Within city walls now, but outside walls in Jesus' time (proven archaeologically)
- Constantine's church built here AD 326 based on early Christian memory
- Evidence of limestone quarry with tombs (typical execution site)

**Garden Tomb:**
- Rock-cut tomb discovered 1867
- Dating: First Temple period (8th-7th century BC) - too early for Jesus
- Pleasant garden setting
- Skull-shaped hill nearby ("Gordon's Calvary")
- Resembles Gospel description
- Good for reflection but archaeologically incorrect date

**Biblical Requirements:**
- Outside city walls
- Near a garden
- New tomb cut in rock
- Large stone could be rolled across entrance
- Near place of crucifixion

**Scripture References:**
- John 19:17 - "Carrying his own cross, he went out to the place of the Skull (which in Aramaic is called Golgotha)"
- John 19:20 - "The place where Jesus was crucified was near the city, and the sign was written in Aramaic, Latin and Greek"
- John 19:41-42 - "At the place where Jesus was crucified, there was a garden, and in the garden a new tomb, in which no one had ever been laid. Because it was the Jewish day of Preparation and since the tomb was nearby, they laid Jesus there"
- Matthew 27:60 - "and placed it in his own new tomb that he had cut out of the rock. He rolled a big stone in front of the entrance to the tomb"
- Mark 16:3-4 - "Who will roll the stone away from the entrance of the tomb?"

**Archaeological Consensus:** Church of the Holy Sepulchre is more archaeologically credible due to correct dating and location outside 1st-century walls.

**Time Period:** AD 30 or 33 (date of crucifixion)

### Paul's Missionary Journey Sites

#### 17. Theatres and Public Buildings in Biblical Cities
**Locations:** Various Mediterranean cities
**Biblical Context:** Places Paul preached and encountered opposition

**Notable Examples:**

**Theatre at Ephesus:**
- Seated 25,000 people
- Site of riot against Paul (Acts 19)
- Still standing and well-preserved today
- Built into hillside
- Where silversmiths' guild protested Paul's preaching

**Scripture:**
- Acts 19:28-29 - "When they heard this, they were furious and began shouting: 'Great is Artemis of the Ephesians!' Soon the whole city was in an uproar. The people seized Gaius and Aristarchus, Paul's traveling companions from Macedonia, and all of them rushed into the theater together"
- Acts 19:31 - "Even some of the officials of the province, friends of Paul, sent him a message begging him not to venture into the theater"

**Areopagus (Mars Hill), Athens:**
- Ancient court and public meeting place
- Rock outcrop northwest of Acropolis
- Where Paul preached to Stoic and Epicurean philosophers
- Traditional site of Athenian judicial proceedings

**Scripture:**
- Acts 17:19-22 - "Then they took him and brought him to a meeting of the Areopagus, where they said to him, 'May we know what this new teaching is that you are presenting?'"
- Acts 17:22-34 - Paul's famous sermon: "People of Athens! I see that in every way you are very religious. For as I walked around and looked carefully at your objects of worship, I even found an altar with this inscription: TO AN UNKNOWN GOD"

**Caesarea Maritima:**
- Built by Herod the Great
- Roman administrative center
- Pilate's headquarters
- Where Paul was imprisoned for two years
- Theatre, hippodrome, palace, aqueduct all excavated

**Scripture:**
- Acts 23:23-35 - Paul transferred to Caesarea
- Acts 24:27 - "When two years had passed, Felix was succeeded by Porcius Festus"
- Acts 25:23 - "The next day Agrippa and Bernice came with great pomp and entered the audience room with the high-ranking military officers and the prominent men of the city"
- Acts 26:1-32 - Paul's defense before Agrippa

**Corinth:**
- Bema (judgment seat) where Paul stood trial before Gallio
- Erastus inscription (city treasurer, Romans 16:23)
- Meat market (1 Corinthians 10:25)
- Extensive excavations of Roman-era city

**Scripture:**
- Acts 18:12-17 - "While Gallio was proconsul of Achaia, the Jews of Corinth made a united attack on Paul and brought him to the place of judgment"

**Time Period:** First century AD (Roman period)

## Summary: What Archaeological Sites Reveal

### Validation of Biblical Accounts

Archaeological sites consistently validate Scripture:

1. **Geographic Accuracy** - Places described in the Bible actually existed where the Bible says they did
2. **Architectural Details** - Descriptions of buildings, gates, water systems match archaeological findings
3. **Cultural Context** - Sites reveal customs, practices, and daily life matching biblical descriptions
4. **Chronological Accuracy** - Destruction layers, building projects, and occupation periods align with biblical timeline
5. **Prophetic Fulfillment** - Destroyed sites confirm prophetic warnings (e.g., Temple destruction)

### Sites That Were Once Doubted

Critics once claimed these didn't exist or were mislocated:
- Pool of Bethesda (found with five porticoes as described)
- Pool of Siloam (rediscovered 2004)
- Nazareth (house from Jesus' time discovered)
- Pilate's Caesarea (Pilate Stone found)
- Cities like Capernaum, Magdala, Bethsaida (all located and excavated)

### The Pattern of Archaeological Confirmation

When archaeological excavation occurs at biblical sites:
- Biblical descriptions are confirmed accurate
- Timeline matches biblical chronology
- Details once thought "too specific to be true" prove precisely accurate
- Sites "invented" by critics turn out to be real

### Conclusion

"The stone the builders rejected has become the cornerstone" (Psalm 118:22). Archaeological sites bear silent witness to the historical accuracy of Scripture. From Jericho to Jerusalem, from Hazor to Capernaum, from Megiddo to the Pool of Siloam, the spade of the archaeologist consistently confirms the Word of God.

While we walk by faith and not by sight, believers can be encouraged that the biblical narrative is firmly rooted in real history, real places, and real events. The stones indeed cry out (Luke 19:40).
