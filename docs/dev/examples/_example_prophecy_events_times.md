# Prophecy Events and Times

This study explores the sequence and timing of major prophetic events, particularly focusing on end-times prophecy and how different biblical passages relate to the timeline of Christ's return.

## Old Testament Foundation

The foundation of biblical prophecy begins in the Old Testament, with books like Daniel, Isaiah, and Ezekiel providing detailed descriptions of future events.

### Key Themes

- **Messianic Prophecy**: Predictions about the coming Messiah
- **Kingdom Timeline**: The establishment of God's eternal kingdom
- **Restoration**: The return of Israel to prominence

## New Testament Fulfillment

The New Testament reveals the fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies and provides additional details about end-times events.

### The Olivet Discourse

Jesus provided prophecies in Matthew 24, Mark 13, and Luke 21 describing signs of the end times.

### Pauline Eschatology

Paul wrote extensively about the future in 1 Thessalonians 4-5 and 2 Thessalonians, discussing the rapture and the Day of the Lord.

## Contemporary Interpretation

Different theological traditions interpret the timing and sequence of events differently:

- **Preterism**: Events already occurred
- **Historicism**: Ongoing fulfillment through history
- **Futurism**: Future fulfillment of most prophecies
- **Idealism**: Spiritual fulfillment rather than literal

## Timeline Models

### Pretribulational

Rapture before 7-year tribulation period

### Midtribulational

Rapture at the midpoint of tribulation

### Posttribulational

Rapture after tribulation, before God's wrath

## Application

Understanding prophecy should:
- Increase faith and hope
- Motivate holy living
- Encourage watchfulness
- Reduce fear of future events

> The purpose of prophecy is not to satisfy curiosity, but to transform lives and draw us closer to God.
